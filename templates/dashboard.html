Welcome to Fake Indian Currency Detection


Welcome to the Fake Indian Currency Detection website, dedicated to combating the proliferation of counterfeit banknotes in circulation. Counterfeiting poses a significant threat to financial systems and undermines public trust in currency. Our platform equips individuals and businesses with tools to identify and distinguish genuine Indian currency notes from counterfeit replicas. Through advanced image processing and machine learning technologies, we empower users to detect subtle security features and intricate designs unique to legitimate banknotes.


In the realm of currency security, the ₹ 500, ₹ 200, and ₹ 100 denominations stand as pillars of financial integrity. These banknotes integrate sophisticated anti-counterfeit measures, including watermarking, security threads, and microprinting, to thwart unauthorized replication. Our website offers an immersive exploration of these security elements, unraveling the intricacies of each denomination's design. By understanding the nuanced characteristics of authentic currency, users gain essential knowledge to safeguard against financial fraud and illicit activities.

Currency Information


₹ 500 Note




The ₹ 500 denomination banknotes in the Mahatma Gandhi (New) Series bear signature of the Governor, Reserve Bank of India. The note has motif of "Red Fort" on reverse, depicting country's cultural heritage. The base colour of the note is "Stone Grey". The note has other designs and geometric patterns aligning with the overall colour scheme, both at obverse and reverse.
The size of the note is 66mm x 150mm
